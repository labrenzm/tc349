Card Sort for the Moon Website!

CARD SORTING

First Study: NASA's Moon Website

Card Sorting is a very useful tool to help design the information hierarchy of your website. It can help you understand how a user interprets your website, and where things should be placed or grouped. For NASA's moon website, 4 card sorts were done; one by me, and 3 others by some of my friends. I chose to display the data from the 2 sorts that differed the most:

My process for this was as follows: Every category and sub-category item had an index card. I asked my friends to group items into categories, with one card forming the category head. The data from these 2 that differed the most showed many differences.

  • Firstly, I noticed that the Get Involved card was often a Category Header with many things listed under it, or it was under the News and Events.  It was one that was hardly consistent between the 4 sorts.
  • Secondly, there were many words which got put into different Category heads, or sub categories.  Some of these were:
    • LADEE Launch
    • Features
    • @yourLibrary
    • Many of the Strategic Partners
    • Moon Toolkit

Some conclusions that I made about this data is that for one, some people may not be able to tell the difference from a strategic partner, to something that they could be involved in.  I'm not sure what could be done about that besides the user knowing more about the programs and what exactly there are and what they do.   Also, many of the sorts had a few slight variances under the About the Moon Category head, mainly interchanging them with the News and Events Category Head.
